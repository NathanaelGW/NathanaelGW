Slovenia

Tolmin Gorge and Dante's Grotto

In the southernmost tip of Slovenia's Triglav National Park is Tolmin Gorge. You can descend down steps into this alluring canyon with its azure waters, moss covered cliffs and mysterious tunnels. The trip here is worth the surreal beauty of Tolmin Gorge alone, but another enticing adventure exists. Follow the path to Dante’s Grotto, a 1140 meter long cave system of twisting, labrynthian passageways. This cave is not recommended for tourists without a guide, but if you have the caving expertise, make sure you're prepared before you give it a go.

Route

How to Get There


From the capital city of Ljubljana, Slovenia you’ll have to drive 2 hours west to the town of Tolmin. Taking the local transit will add an extra hour to your trip and multiple transfers. However, it may be a good opportunity to stop by Lake Bled on the way. Tolmin Gorge is only 2km north out of town with a traditional, Slovenian restaurant right near the trail head. A bus runs from Tolmin town to Tolmin Gorge during July and August.

Tolmin Gorge Trail


The entrance fee to Tolmin Gorge is 6 euros. The trail is 4km long and is of easy difficulty. There are steps leading down into the gorge and the path follows the Tolminka River. To make it a loop, you can cross the river by bridge and walk up out of the gorge to the road. From there you can walk over Devil’s Bridge giving you excellent views of the gorge, and then continue along the road back to the parking lot. If you decide to visit Dante’s Grotto, turn right instead of left towards the bridge once you get out of the gorge.

Dantes Grotto


Dante’s Grotto is named after the 14th century poet, Dante Aligheri. It is said that the cave in Tolmin inspired Dante’s poem, The Divine Comedy, which depicts his descent into Hell. The first section of Dante’s Grotto was once made more accessible to tourists but no longer. Remnants of old electrical equipment lay scattered and abandoned. Luckily, carved steps and hand holds make progress through the cave a bit easier. You still must climb steep inclines and it can be easy to get lost. Arrows painted on the rock can help you find your way out if need be. The cave complex is 1140 meters long, 42 meters deep and boasts three main halls. Tourists are not recommended to explore the cave without a competent guide.

Gear Needed

There is no gear required for Tolmin Gorge other than a map and a good stick to steady yourself over slippery rocks.


With Dante’s Grotto, however, more preparation and skills are necessary. Before you enter the cave, make sure you have a headlamp, an extra light, extra batteries, a helmet, a map of the cave system, water and some food. Make sure you are wearing boots with good grip as there is some climbing involved and the rocks are slippery.

Tips

  • Wear non-cotton clothes as cotton will soak up the abundance of moisture in the cave and chill you to the bone.
  • Mark your path out of the cave as you go deeper. Do not panic if you feel lost.
  • It’s easy to panic feeling trapped and claustrophobic. Calmly assess your situation and find your way out.
  • Stay dry, hypothermia is one of the major dangers while exploring a cave system.
  • Conserve your light.
  • Don’t enter the cave alone.
