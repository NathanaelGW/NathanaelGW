Estonia

Viru Bog in Lahemaa

Bog-walking has become a popular experience in Estonia. Stroll throught the eerie atmosphere of Viru Bog in Lahemaa National Park to see multicolored lichens and various wildlife. Walk on wooden walkways in order not to disturb the sensitive fauna and keep your feet dry. Climb up the tower to get great views of the scenery. Get out of your comfort zone and do as the locals do, swim in the bog!

Route

How to Get There


Fly into Tallinn, capital of Estonia. Lahemaa Park lies 52 kilometers east of Tallinn off of highway E20. Buses leave from Tallinn every hour and you can get off at Loksa tee stop before venturing into the bog.


View Full Map

Viru Bog Trail


There's a trailhead near the highway (E20) off of road 85 on the south side of the park. There's also an entrance from the north side of the park off of Joelahtme-Kemba Road (260). The whole loop through the bog is 6km through easy terrain.


View Full Map

Gear Needed

There is no special gear required to hike Viru Bog. Insect Repellant might be necessary in the spring. Boardwalks cover the wet parts of the trail so you won't need rubber boots, sneakers or hiking boots will do. Bring a swimsuit if you dare to jump in the bog water!

Tips

  • Stay on the path! A single footprint in the bog can take 7 years to grow back! Don't disturb the flora and fauna.
  • Bring enough water for your walk. Bring a lunch or a snack to eat along the way.
  • The viewtower can only hold up to 30 people.
  • DO NOT LITTER!
  • Beware slippery boardwalks in the winter
