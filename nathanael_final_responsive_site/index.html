Landsailing in Argentina

Millions of years ago, the lake in Pampa del Leoncito dried up leaving a flat plain behind. Argentinians in the nearby village of Barreal had no choice but to improvise by sailing on wheels rather than water. With nothing to obstruct the wind whipping across the ancient lake bed, 'land yachts' can reach up to speeds of 100 km per hour! Ride the winds amidst the surreal, moon-like landscape of Pampa del Leoncito.

Caving in Thrihnukagigur

Interested in descending into a volcano? Have no fear, Iceland's volcano Thrihnukagigur hasn't erupted in 4000 years. Rappel 700 feet into the belly of this slumbering beast and admire its geological beauty. The magma chamber is so large it could hold the Statue of Liberty! If rappelling down a rope doesn't suit your fancy, Thrihnukagigur is the only volcano in the world with an elevater to take you down into its magma chamber.

Climb Machu Picchu

Explore the remnants of the Inca Empire in the heart of Peru. Take a five day epic journey along the Inca Trail, built as a highway for that ancient empire. Start your hike at the Sacred Valley and wind your way up to the Incan capital city of Machu Pichu. Survive the rugged terrain of the Andes Mountains and you will be rewarded with an unforgettable experience.

Halong Bay

The azure waters of Halong Bay, Vietnam, are inviting for boaters and swimmers alike. Marvel at the scenery as you pass countless islands, standing like overgrown, stone towers in the sea.

Fundy Trail

Experience incredible ocean views as you hike along Fundy Trail. This 19 kilometer trail begins just outside St. Martins, New Brunswick and takes you through the gorgeous coastal wilderness.

Ankarana

On the north-eastern tip of Madagascar lies the national park Ankarana. Hike through the variable environments and witness geological phenomena like the ‘tsingies’, a forest of limestone spires.
